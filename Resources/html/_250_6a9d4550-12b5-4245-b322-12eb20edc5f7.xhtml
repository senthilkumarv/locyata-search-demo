Task 4: Configuring and Verifying a Dual-Stack Router
In this task, you will enable IPv4 connectivity between networks that are currently configured for IPv6 on your workgroup router.
Activity Procedure
Complete these steps:
Step1
On your workgroup router, configure the loopback 2 interface with the IPv4 address that is indicated in the table in the Job Aids of this lab activity.
Step2
Use the show ip route command to verify that EIGRP has learned the network of the loopback 2 interface of your partner.
The EIGRP network statement that you configured in a previous lab activity (network 10.0.0.0) should advertise the IPv4 network that is assigned to the loopback 2 interface.
Step3
Ping all the IPv4 addresses of your partner workgroup router, including the loopback 2 interface.
Step4
Ping all the IPv6 addresses of your partner workgroup router, including the loopback 2 interface.
To make it easier to ping the IPv6 addresses for the remainder of this activity, obtain the IPv6 addresses of all the routers in your workgroup and copy them into a Notepad document. You can use the show cdp neighbor detail command to display the IPv6 address of the directly connected interface of your partner router. You can also establish a Telnet session into the router of your partner and use the show ipv6 interface brief command to display the IPv6 addresses of the rest of the interfaces of your partner router. When you have documented the IPv6 addresses, simply copy the address from the Notepad file and paste it into the ping command when you wish to ping the IPv6 interface of one of your neighboring routers.
Step5
Your output should be similar to the following example:
RouterA# ping 2001:410:1:2:216:9DFF:FEB0:EA48
 
 
Type escape sequence to abort.
 
Sending 5, 100-byte ICMP Echos to 2001:410:1:2:216:9DFF:FEB0:EA48, timeout is 2
 
seconds:

Success rate is 100 percent (5/5), round-trip min/avg/max = 28/28/32 ms
Activity Verification
You have completed this activity when you attain this result:
  • You have established IPv4 and IPv6 connectivity between routers in your group.
