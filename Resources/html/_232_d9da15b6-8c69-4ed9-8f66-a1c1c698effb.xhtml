Task 1: Create an Extended ACL to Block Telnet Traffic into Your Workgroup
In this task, you will work with a student in another workgroup. You will configure an extended IP ACL to block incoming Telnet traffic from outside of your workgroup. You will configure the ACL, apply it to an interface, and verify the configuration by having your partner try to establish a Telnet session into your workgroup switch. If you have correctly configured the ACL, the Telnet request should fail. Next, try to ping the same device, which should succeed.
Group assignments: A-B, C-D, E-F, and G-H.
Activity Procedure
Complete these steps on the workgroup router:
Step1
From your PC, establish a connection to the lab equipment.
Step2
Shut down the second serial interface (S0/0/1) of your workgroup router by using the shutdown command.
Step3
Create an IP extended ACL to deny only Telnet traffic into your workgroup.
Step4
Apply the IP extended ACL to your first serial interface.
Should the extended ACL be applied as an inbound or outbound ACL?
_______________________________________________________________________________
Step5
Enter the show ip access-list command to display the content of your IP extended ACL.
Step6
Enter the show ip interface command to verify that the ACL is applied to the first serial interface.
Step7
Ask your partner to establish a Telnet session into your workgroup switch (10.x.x.11) from their workgroup router.
All attempts to use Telnet into your workgroup switch should fail.
Step8
Ask your partner to ping your workgroup switch (10.x.x.11) from their workgroup router.
All traffic into your workgroup devices, except Telnet traffic, should be successful.
Step9
Proceed to Task 2.
Activity Verification
You have completed this task when you attain this result:
  • You have created an IP extended ACL that blocks incoming Telnet traffic but allows all other traffic from outside of your workgroup.
